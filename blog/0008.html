(Please) Hold Me and Tell Me I'm Brave

April 12, 2026 - 1:21 AM

There is something I am deeply afraid of, and I can’t stop it from happening. My decisions, though well-intentioned, feel minuscule underneath the skyscrapers where I stand. As much as I want to be enough, to complete the work with my own hands, it is impossible. Even with the hearts and eyes of others, somehow it is as insurmountable as before, and at times even more so. Pushed into the space between empty parking lots and even emptier office buildings, what else are we to do? I kick. I shout. I scream.

“Is anybody listening?”

The horizon stares back as my voice fractures into pieces, that fracture into pieces, that fracture into pieces. The reflections of what was once a thought I had, sail into the mass of radio fuzz on the outer-reaches of our conceivable world, like a pigeon flying straight into a window. It’s already happened. We’ve been subsumed.

When a tree falls and no one is around to hear it, the tree does make a sound. There is an understood loneliness and longing for connection. This is not a simple problem, but at the very least it is one we can begin to understand. This is not where we find ourselves, however. Our reality is that trees fall every second, making all manner of clashes, tears, and thuds, and no one so much as notices.

In a world where we all are screaming, is a sound made? Or is it a constant and silent subatomic hum, keeping us barely alive, forever destined to be drowned out by a lack of courage to do the right thing? There’s no doubt we’ve been subsumed.

We have fallen beneath the ruling class of algorithms and infrastructure we conjured to increase our experience. In doing so, we have optimized our being out of the picture. Our presence, ultimately too expensive and ugly to maintain, must be recycled. Reduced and reused to drive attention in an environment that festers on division. Attention to beliefs, products, ideas that must be marketed back to us in terms of things we lack.

There is always more to be had, more to be said, more to be seen. Our attention has been dragged and beaten. Dust blown in its face. It’s been subsumed. In our pursuit for something we have given everything. Not a single piece of myself remains that can speak to me. Even if there was a piece left, I’m not sure I’d be able to hear it or understand what it says.

But I have to try.

If there’s any chance that there is something left, something still untouched, then I have to know what it means. I have to know how it feels. I want to feel it too. Then I can say who I am. Even if no one else is able to hear me say it, I can be who I am because I know who I can be. Even if I am subsumed, even if my words are drowned under a murky wave of analog snow, or sealed shut in the arctic code vault, I can rest knowing that my signal was true. No matter the strength or the distance it traveled, my signal was here. It was now, it was me.

So here I am, broadcasting a message toward the stars. My first and possibly only chance to break through. If by happenstance or miracle, your antenna was adjusted correctly, and you’re seeing this now; know that this is me. This is who I am and who I was. Know that, even though we are very far apart, my spirit is here. It always will be, as this is all I have ever wanted. To be here now, in this way, with you. All I ask, is that you see me for what I am, and that you try. If not for me, try for you. Please. Please.

Someone I know is dying. It’s a slow, and painful death. It’s also a death ripe with relief. I feel selfish, almost. When someone dies, it is a chance to be reverent. We are presented a moment to recognize what we had, what we have lost, and what we have left to go. It is a chilling thing. To know that we stared at life in its eyes and it stared back at us. Like twin cities, a highway of light connected us in the places we shared. Now, that light is gone.

I’ve known people who have died before. It was never like this though. Somehow, this time I feel more sober, more aware. I consider myself lucky. I have not lost someone that I really knew, like a partner or a friend. I don’t quite know what that feels like. Soon enough, I will.

I have grieved before. I know what it means to grieve. This time it feels different. This person isn’t dead yet, but maybe they will be by the time you’re reading this. I am grieving so soon and so fast. I’m handling this grief like a task or a process. Something that is done for the sake of getting it over with, rather than having done it because it was what I needed to do.

Then there is this aspect of nostalgia. I am losing something I innately know. It was something I grew up with. Maybe I’m making it out to be more than it was. Through the rose-tinted glasses, it felt simpler, it felt easier, but it didn’t feel like the truth. I always felt like nostalgia was cheating. It was a shortcut to something else. There’s something deeper underneath it.

Then there is the saying goodbye to someone else. I know I’ll never see them again. Right now, it isn’t hard for me to say that, but later I know it will be difficult to recognize this fact. The door will have closed and there will be nothing I can do to open it. As for now, I take for granted my ability to open the door and to shut it as I see fit. It is only when the option is taken away will I recognize how often I used the door and in what ways I rely on it.

I think I’m avoiding it – I’ll stop wasting your time. I am the someone I know that is dying. It is not my physical self but a version of me. The person I used to be.

I think back to the person I was a decade ago and I do not know where he is. I still see parts of him in me. The way I run my hands through my hair and the interests that I had are still here. He will always be a part of me in one way or another, but it is true to me now that he is gone. He has been gone for so long that I am not really sure when he left, or maybe I should say, I am not really sure when I threw him out.

That’s more accurate. As I grew out of the habits that I had and replaced them with ones that were more mature, or modern, I unknowingly tossed younger me out the window. Bit by bit. It is a very selfish thing. Only now, having taken out the trash, do I recognize that some of what I threw away I actually wanted. Maybe I only want them now that I can’t have them, I think that’s part of it. Even if I could have these parts back, I know I wouldn’t want them. They don’t fit me – they aren’t me.

I couldn’t give up what I have now. I have a car and the freedom that comes with knowing I can go most anywhere I want. I have a space that I can call truly my own that houses all of my trinkets and keepsakes. I have relationships that I value beyond belief and reason. I have a mind that I can say is inquisitive and curious, always ready to learn, always reaching for something just beyond my fingertips. My sense of self is so certain and yet so fickle. I used to be so different.

I was bratty and dismissive. Kind at times, but overall not understanding. I had very different beliefs and priorities. I had very certain wants and needs, some of which I have fulfilled and some of which I have adapted into something more true for my present self. I have changed for the better. Even though I am happy to be who I am, I still miss that kid.

On one hand, I feel proud that I was able to do things that I think a younger me would have respected. On the other, I feel guilty that in some sense, I killed him. I solved many of his problems, but in doing so, I took his essence away.

As I look back, I see all the ways in which I have changed. I can say I am happier now. I can say I believe in a world that is better than the one we have come to inherit. I can say that I’m fighting for what I believe is right. I can say that I am trying my best to be kind and understanding. I can say that I am patient and have hope in every aspect. I can say that I believe I am someone better than I once was. I can say that I am still changing and I will continue to evolve.

But like a butterfly that just emerged from its chrysalis or the sun that has just dipped below the tides, I look back at this corpse. This hollow gray husk which I used to be. I recognize I am at this turning point. I am about to turn away and realize something greater. On this brief dot, in the timeline of the universe, I can’t bring myself to leave just yet. I hold him in my arms. I still remember those things that we did. We were there together, I just didn’t know it yet.

I remember the stories we were told, the food we ate, the sleepless nights we had, the wounds we were given, the holes we dug. Here now, divorced from your existence, I hold this gray thing close. I see him now, not as me, but as another human. Those pains that I then pushed aside now appear more obvious and real than ever before. These same pains that I have tried day after day to heal.

As I continue to hold you, knowing that I am about to leave, know that I see you. I hear you. I feel you. You wanted someone to understand you. As I stand at this juncture, I know that it’s too late to say something, but I’m not sure I can let go without saying it.

Dear younger me,

In equal parts, as I have grown up, I have also gotten older. I have realized so much and have so much more to learn. I have recognized my mistakes and know there are ever more to correct. But it is in your strength that I have built this new foundation today.

I know you were scared. Scared of being forgotten, scared of never being understood, scared of never finding your own place. While those fears are still alive in me today, I can still thank you. Though you were scared, you persevered. More than that, you were brave. I know everything you have faced and you were so, so brave.

You’ve built me up from nothing, and for this life I now live, I am forever grateful. I wish I could give you something. Some kind of reward or small gesture – anything to make your absence feel resolved. As I hold you still, I wonder what you would say.

I know now what you wanted. I know now why you lashed out. I know now why you drowned yourself in spite and loathing. You were alone in a world that didn’t want you. You were asked to stand tall without being taught how. You fought the only fight you were capable of fighting. I don’t blame you.

You believed in a future that was certain and inevitable. While I don’t live in the future you wanted, I do live in the present we have. A present that you worked tirelessly to create. Day after day you put in your best effort. Though you have left this world with goals unmet, you have set me up to continue your work.

These goals have changed with me, as I have grown into someone else. If you were still here and you took my hand one last time, I would show them to you. I would show you what I am hoping for and what I am working towards. I think you would love it. I think you would be proud to know that we’ve built something that is our own. Something that is both equal parts you and me.

At times, I’ve hated you. I saw you as too stupid or too selfish. Too short-sighted to have relied on. You were brash and too confident for your own good. You didn’t prioritize the things that I wish you had. You made so many mistakes.

But with all your faults, you were enough. I put you down because I was ashamed of you, of us. That was not fair of me. I am sorry.

I wish we could start over, but your time is up and this is goodbye.

I cannot continue to see the world as you do. I have new responsibilities. I have my own hopes and dreams. I still know what you felt when I look at the things that you saw. I still feel them, though their power fades over time. I am different now. I can choose to feel something new, and many times I do.

I am feeling this loss, but it is not like other loss that I have known. It is more akin to an honoring or a recognition of the life you leave behind. I am given this time, to walk through all that you have. There is so much time in you. I can only be astounded by what we’ve become.

As you continue to fade and I remember what you have taught me, I seek to build a better self. A self that is built on the back of your experiences. I recognize your struggles and the places that you failed. I seek to pick up the torch and carry on where you left off, so in return for your persistence, I leave you these promises:

I promise to be earnest – to tell the truth and create boundaries in which I feel safe.

I promise to be kind – to show love and understanding unconditionally to myself and to others.

I promise to be brave – to stand up for what is right and to be the voice that is needed.

I promise to be strong – to give my all whenever I am able and for whoever might need it.

And finally, I promise to remember you – to know how you felt and know that what you felt was true, because together, I will always be a little more me, as long as I have you.

It is okay, Jackson. You can rest easy now. I am so excited for you to see what I create. I am going to take good care of us. <3

...

Before you go, I just wanted to say that you are appreciated. Your hard work does not go unnoticed. You have been strong for so long. You are valuable and provide meaning to this world, just for being who you are. You are trying your best. You are enough. There is someone in your life who needs to hear this too, please go tell them. Thanks again for kicking it, I’ll see you soon.
